About the Project

What is Project Restoration?

All of the volunteers after a successful day of planting Valley Oaks at Willett.

Project Restoration

Project Restoration is focused on planting drought tolerant trees and educating my community about what drought tolerance is. It is the culmination of my completed Gold Award, which includes planting trees at my local elementary school, Willett Elementary, creating this website, and printing brochures about what trees are drought tolerant. I began by mapping out what I wanted to do, and then began to contact local businesses that very generously sponsored my project in full. After lots of preparation, I rallied some volunteers and held my very first planting day. We planted three native Californian Valley Oaks, which are very drought tolerant and suitable for Willett. In the future, I aspire to plant more trees.

I hope that my project will help in making the city of Davis more resilient to the drought and its inhabitants better informed. My goal is not only to plant drought-resistant trees but also to offer information in the form of brochures and through this website.

Background

This project was inspired as a result of the recent drought in Central Valley. Many people are aware of the drought, but not the extent of its severity.
