Gold Award

What is a Gold Award?

The Gold Award is the highest achievement available in Girl Scouting. It is a prestigious award which recognizes girls for doing their part in changing the world. The award requires an 80+ hour project with seven steps. Gold Award recipients are girls who are passionate about their topic, and are willing to share their ideas, passions, and time with their respective communities.

History of the Gold Award

The Gold Award is historic in Girl Scouting. It was first awarded in 1916, and was called “The Golden Eagle of Merit”. This started the longstanding tradition of recognizing girls who make a difference in their communities with the award. As of 1980, the name was changed to simply “The Gold Award”, and has stuck with the name ever since.
